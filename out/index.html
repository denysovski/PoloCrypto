Position Sizing Strategy

The 2% Rule and Risk Management

Professional traders follow the 2% rule: never risk more than 2% of your total capital on a single trade. This means if you have $10,000, your maximum loss per trade should be $200. This approach protects your portfolio from catastrophic losses during inevitable downturns. Calculate your position size by dividing your risk amount by the distance to your stop-loss. For example, if you're willing to lose $200 and your stop is $500 away, you trade 0.4 BTC. This mathematical discipline separates successful traders from gamblers who chase volatile moves.

How to Gain

A 4-phase strategy cycle showing exactly when to buy, hold, add, and sell for maximum returns. Structure beats emotion.

Phase 1

Weeks 1-4

Accumulation

Building Your Position Silently

Watch for coins consolidating after a decline. Volume should be low with bounces off support. This is when whales silently buy without pumping the price. Look for daily closes above key moving averages (50-day, 200-day). Enter on dip buys with tight stops below the consolidation range. Size each position at 1-2% of portfolio. Don't rushthe best entry signals take time to form. Use limit orders to buy gradually, averaging in over 2-3 weeks rather than one lump sum.

KEY METRIC TO WATCH:

Volume below 50% of 30-day average = Safe accumulation

Is Crypto Really
for You?

An honest evaluation framework to determine if you're ready for cryptocurrency. Most people aren't, and that's okay.

Financial Readiness

Crypto is only for money you can afford to lose completely. If it disappears, would it impact your lifestyle? If yes, don't invest yet. Build emergency savings first. You need stable income because crypto requires patience through downturnsyou can't panic sell if you need the money next month. A $5k investment on $50k income is risky; on $500k income it's prudent. Context matters.
